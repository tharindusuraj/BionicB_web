18 April 2021

Drones are used to perform tasks in agriculture, transportation, security and surveillance, photography etc . In order for a drone to perform a specific task, it must be configured to do so. Drone programming is crucial in fulfilling the realization of fully autonomous drones as human pilots will not suffice due to the huge demand in the industry.

There are two different levels of drone programming: low level and high level. Low-level drone programming concentrates on the basic functionalities such as maintaining the stability, checking the battery status, calculating the travelling speeds etc. Low level programming is mandatory for both human piloted and autopilot drones. High-level drone programming concentrates on the applications of the drone, where the drone gets commands from its flight computer such as fly point A to B, rotate the camera, increase the altitude etc.

Programming of drones allows drones to fly without human intervention making decisions on their own, taking into consideration situational and self-awareness. Different types of sensors are used by the flight computer to understand the situation of the environment. The inbuilt software of the drone makes decisions based on the sensor data which figures the condition of the surrounding environment. With the increasing demand for drones in several industries, drone programming allows a single user to handle thousands of drones at once. Further increasing level of accuracy, transparency, low cost and quality assurance are some key benefits of programmed drones. As a result of this, drone programming skills have become more in demand in the future job market..
